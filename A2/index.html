What Energy do we Use?

From the stacked area chart on the left, you can clearly see that majority of our electricity is generated from coal, gas, hydroelectric and oil. Out of all 4 only hydroelectric is renewable and sustainable. Nuclear power only generates about 1/3 of other mahor electric sources currently. The rate of nuclear energy usage increases slow and is not projected to be overtaking any of these major electric source.

From the map above, you can compare the increase in electricity generated from nuclear over time comparing to other countries. Currently,United States of America is clearly the biggest utiliser of nuclear energy. Historically they have been the biggest users of nuclear energy. China overtook Russia as the third

largest utiliser of nuclear energy ever since 2016. However it can be seen that France is the second largest user of nuclear energy. Reasons for these rankings can be due to multiple reasons. United States and Russia have had headstarts compared to other countries, where they undergo extensive

research for nuclear weapons during WW2. These research then helped their nuclear energy research later on. China has the 2nd largest population on earth, meaning they naturally need to generate more energy, and for France nuclear energy is the cheapest energy compared to fossil fuels.

The current situation for nuclear energy is however not good. Projected nuclear capacity is to decline significantly over the years. This can be predicted as there are few plans to build more nuclear power plants, and more nuclear plants are being retired, even after numerous nuclear life extension programs. This is especially true in europe, where Germany has announce its plan to retire all nuclear plants.

From the stacked normalised bar chart above, you can see that majority of nuclear plants in USA and EU are over 30 years. This shows that they are getting their lifetime extended, and eventually will have to be retired. China currently has the youngest plants as their nuclear energy plan started much later. Aside from them, this is a worrying sign that nuclear energy may die out.

But why use nuclear energy? This is because nuclear energy does not produce CO2 emissions when producing electricity. Hence it is crucial for it to exist to support the emerging renewable energy such as Solar and Wind energy. The stacked area chart on the left shows how much CO2 emissions have been avoided compared to other non-renewable energies.
